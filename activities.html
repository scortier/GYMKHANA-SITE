Kabaddi and Kho-Kho

NIT Silchar boasts of a very rich sporting culture in Kabaddi and Kho-Kho, having a well maintained clay court of internationally approved dimensions, regular inter hostel and inter competitions are being organized in the premises. A college Kabaddi team is particularly trained and prepared for representing the college in various inter-college competitions like Inter-NIT Sports Meet, where the team members are selected from the best Kabaddi players of the college. Regular Kabaddi sessions are organized by the Hockey & Kabaddi Secretary of the Gymkhana Studentsâ€™ Union Body under the guidance of Sports Secretary and Sports Officer
The college team is a well-crafted and rigorously trained team on an amateur level, consisting of good raiders, defenders and all-rounders. The freshmen students are taught about the basics of the games right from the beginning with practice matches being held on regular intervals to check the growth the students in the sports.

Athletics and Gym

One of the most vibrant clubs of NIT Silchar is its Athletics and Gymnastics Club, conducting several thrilling events on workout and field & track related sports. This club is responsible for conducting fitness events like body building competition, powerlifting, and arm wrestling along with athletics events like sprinting, marathon run, javelin throw, shot put throw, discus throw and cycling; throughout the year. Recently, this club has started Yoga training in the campus which attracted a lot of participation.

Independence Day

NITS being the abode of patriotic souls, celebrated the 69th Independence day with great zeal and enthusiasm. The tricolor was hoisted by Director Sir, followed by his inspiring speech. Events that followed included the marvellous parade by the NCC cadets that set a remarkable example of synchronization.

Gandhi Jayanti

The 2nd of October, 2019 stood witness to a plethora of events and activities on the auspicious occasion of Gandhi Jayanti and Lal Bahadur Shashtri Jayanti. The string of events started of early morning with the pledge taking ceremony followed by cycle rally to SMCH, where a Nukkad Natak was acted out by our students. In the later part of the day a cleanliness drive was organised in our campus followed by a series of literary events like the essay writing, debate, PPT presentation and painting competition. A workshop on solar energy was also conducted for the students of nearby schools. The day ended with a cultural evening where monoact, singing and dancing competitions were organised.

General Fresher's

A breathtaking and amazing night was put up by the Gymkhana Union body to welcome the freshmen to the college. The program unleashed overwhelming fun and excitement by the awe inspiring performances of the students. The most awaited " Mr. & Miss Fresher' s " competition was also conducted on that day itself.

Janmashtami

The festival of the birthday of Lord Krishna, Janmashtami, was celebrated on 5th September with great enthusiasm. A puja for Lord Krishna was organized on that auspicious day in Hostel -7, which was followed by the most exciting " Dahi-Handi" competition, which involves the breaking of an earthen pot raised to a considerable height. The zeal and energy of the students was vividly demonstrated in the competition where all the hostels competed against each other. All the faculty members and students were invited to be a part of the grand celebration.

Durga Puja

To seek the blessings of the Goddess of strength, Maa Durga, a 3-day celebration of Durga Puja was organised by the combined effort of the Durga Puja Committee and Gymkhana Students' Union Body from 21st to 23rd of october with great pomp and show. The Durga Puja revelry was not only limited to the holy rituals, but also extended to the various cultural programmes including the exuberant " dandiya night" in which all the faculty members and students participated wholeheartedly with immense pleasure.The 3 days of extravaganza ended with the Idol immersion and bidding farewell to Maa Durga on the way to her heavenly abode.

Diwali

Diwali, one of the most auspicious festivals of India, celebrates the victory of good over evil. The festival of light spreads happiness, prosperity and well being of everyone. In NITS, we celebrated the festival of light with great enthusiasm and love. All the boarders of each and every hostel celebrated the auspicious occasion with great excitement. The whole campus of NIT Silchar was glowing with the shine of the magnificent diyas and colourful lights, on the pristine occasion of Diwali which was celebrated on the 11th of November. On that day the students got actively involved in making beautiful Rangolies and decorating their hostels thereby creating an amazing festive mood throughout the campus. This year GH-2 took the initiative and organised the Kali Puja in which the whole of NIT Silchar was invited, to witness the ceremony and seek the blessings of Goddess Kali.

Republic Day

Like every other year, our institute celebrated 67th Republic Day on 26th January, 2017. The exuberance and the patriotism of the entire fraternity filled the atmosphere. The event began with an inspiring, kindling speech by Honourable Director Sir followed by a colourful parade by the hostels. The NCC parade aroused patriotism in everyone. Several cultural programs also followed up. ASME also organised a photo exhibition of Indian scientists and their contributions since the ancient ages, depicting the scientific progress of our nation.

Ekta Daud

Rashtriya Ekta Diwas is observed every year in India on 31st October on the occasion of birth anniversary of the famous leader Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, to celebrate the unity and integrity of this vibrant nation. A marathon was organized in the college by Gymkhana Union body on October 31, 2017. Huge number of students, faculties and non-teaching staff participated in the marathon thereby depicting unity and harmony among them. The winners of the marathon were awarded with cash prizes.

Blood Donation Camp

A blood donation camp was set up on the Independence Day by the Gymkhana Students' Union body with the co-operation of the NCC students. Honorable Director Sir, faculty members and a large number of students, faculties and non-teaching staff came forward to donate blood henceforth contributing to the noble cause.
